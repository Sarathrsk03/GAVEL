Under the Indian Child Welfare Act:
 The parent or Indian custodian has the right to intervene in the proceedings.
 The child’s Indian tribe has the right to intervene at any time in a State court proceeding for the foster care
placement of or termination of a parental right.
 If the Indian parent(s) or, if applicable, Indian custodian(s) is unable to afford counsel based on a determination
of indigency by the court, counsel will be appointed to represent the parent or Indian custodian where
authorized by State law.
 The parent, Indian custodian, and Tribe have the right to be granted, upon request, a specific amount of
additional time (up to 20 additional days) to prepare for the proceedings due to circumstances of the particular
case.
 The parent, Indian custodian, and Tribe have the right to petition the court for transfer of the proceeding to
tribal court under 25 U.S.C. 1911, absent objection by either parent: Provided, that such transfer is subject to
declination by the tribal court.
